# 澳洲租房解答：包 Bills、包家具值多少？房价是否合理？
# Australia Rental Guide: Are Bills and Furniture Included Worth It? Is the Rent Fair?

## Q1：PW 是什么意思？为什么租金都写 pw？
在澳洲租房广告里，你经常会看到"pw"。这是 per week 的缩写，意思是"每周"。因为澳洲的租金都是按周来计算的，而不是像国内或其他地区常见的按月计费，所以在看房的时候一定要理解 weekly rent 的概念。

## Q1: What does "PW" mean? Why is the rent always written as "pw"?
In Australian rental ads, "PW" means per week. Unlike many countries where rent is calculated monthly, in Australia it's always listed weekly. International students should remember that "weekly rent" is the standard.

## Q2: How much are Bills and furniture worth?
This is one of the most common concerns among international students. When a listing says "bills included" or "furnished," is it worth it? Let's break it down step by step.

### Bills 大概值多少？
Bills 一般包括 网费 + 水电煤：
- 网费（Internet）：70–90 AUD/月，2人均摊折合约 10 AUD/周。
- 水电煤（Utilities）：70–110 AUD/月/人，折合约 20 AUD/周。
- 合计：约 30 AUD/周/人。

也就是说，如果房东包 bills，相当于帮你承担了约 30 刀/周的开销。

### How much are Bills approximately worth?
Bills typically include Internet + utilities:
- Internet: AUD 70–90 per month, about AUD 10/week if shared by two.
- Utilities (electricity, gas, water): AUD 70–110 per person per month, about AUD 20/week.
- Total: around AUD 30 per week per person.

So if bills are included, you're saving about AUD 30/week.

### 家具大概值多少？
按照家具购置成本 2000 AUD/年计算，每周折合约 40 AUD。
- 主要大件包括：洗衣机、冰箱、床+床垫、桌椅、餐桌。二手家具成本更低，而且毕业时还能二手转卖回收部分费用。

### How much is the furniture worth approximately?
Furniture costs are roughly AUD 2000 per year, or about AUD 40 per week. This includes essentials like washing machine, fridge, bed, desk, and dining table. Buying second-hand is cheaper, and you can resell items when moving out.

### 小提示：谁更常提供包 Bills/家具？
在澳洲，华人中介的房源更可能是"包 bills + 家具"，而 本地房东或正规房源平台的房子往往是不带家具、不含 bills 的。对于预算敏感的留学生，自己租 unfurnished 房源、签 utilities，可能更划算。

### Tip: Who provides Bills/ furniture packages more often?
In Australia, Chinese-run agencies often provide "furnished + bills included" rentals, while local listings are mostly unfurnished and exclude bills. For students on a budget, renting unfurnished and managing utilities yourself may save money.
